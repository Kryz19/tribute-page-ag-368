George Washington

First President of the United States

Timeline

  • 1732 - Born in Westmoreland County, Virginia.
  • 1743 - Washington’s father dies. He inherited Ferry Farm.
  • 1754 - Became a Lieutenant Colonel during the French and Indian War and second-in-command of the 300 Virginia Regiment.
  • 1755 - Washington volunteers to serve as an aide to British General Edward Braddock.
  • 1758 - Washington is elected to Virginia’s House of Burgesses thus his career in politics begin.
  • 1759 - At 26, Washington married Martha Dandridge Custis.
  • 1765 - Washington is elected once again to the House of Burgesses.
  • 1774 - Washington appointed as a one of the seven delegates to the First Continental Congress.
  • 1775 - Washington was appointed as a delegate to the Second Continental Congress.
  • 1775 - Congress created the Continental Army and appointed Washington to be the Commander in Chief.
  • 1776 - Using the element of surprise, Washington and his troops crossed the frozen Delaware River and struck the unsuspecting Hessian mercenaries.
  • 1781 - The Continental Army led by Washington cornered the British Army which led to the Siege of Yorktown.
  • 1781 - Great Britain surrenders.
  • 1781 - Washington attended the Constitutional Convention.
  • 1787 - Washington attended the Constitutional Convention.
  • 1789 - Unanimously Elected as the first President of the United States of America.
  • 1789 - Washington delivered his First Inaugural Address.
  • 1790 - Washington moves to Philadelphia, the new capital of the United States.
  • 1791 - Washington signed the Bank Act which created the first bank in the United States.
  • 1793 - Washington issues the Proclamation of Neutrality to keep the U.S. out of the War between France and Britain.
  • 1796 - Washington published his Farewell Address announcing his retirement.
  • 1797 - After two terms, Washington retired from presidency.
  • 1797 - Washington returns to Mount Vernon.
  • 1799 - George Washington passed away December 14, 1799 due to Epiglottis, a throat infection.
  • 1799 - Buried in the Washington Family Vault at Mount Vernon.
  • 1869 - Washington’s Portrait was issued on the dollar bill.

"To be prepared for war is one of the most effectual means of preserving peace."

- George Washington’s First Annual Address to both Houses of Congress | Friday, January 08, 1790
